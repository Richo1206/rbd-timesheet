Google Maps Travel Tool
How it works:
  • Fully automatic. Every field in this section triggers a background Google Maps lookup as soon as you tab away — no button to press. Travel Hrs/Mins and any ticked km allowances (TK1, TK2, Kms Company Car) fill themselves in a second or two.
  • Select your start location — Devonport Office, Hobart Office, or Custom. If Custom, start typing the address or place name and pick from the Google Autocomplete dropdown (accommodation, other RBD office, meeting point, etc.).
  • Start typing the destination (site name or address) and pick from the Google Autocomplete dropdown. If the place you need isn't listed, type a street address or town + state instead.
  • Tap Verify Route to open Google Maps in a new tab and visually sanity-check the route Google is using.
  • The calculator uses Travel Hrs/Mins (from the morning leg) to auto-set your "Arrived on Site" time based on when you left.
  • TK1 / TK2: tick these in Extra Allowances and the km fields populate automatically using Google's distances. A "Return" section appears where you select where you're going back to (e.g. accommodation, different office). The return origin is always the last site in your chain — no need to type it in. For TK2 multi-site days, click Add another site to chain up to 5 sites (Dest → Site 2 → Site 3 → …).
  • Manual overrides are always preserved — if you type your own Travel Hrs/Mins or km values, auto-fires won't clobber them.

Time Entries
How to fill this in:
  • Left Workshop/Accom — the time you left the workshop or accommodation.
  • Arrived on Site — the time you arrived at the job site.
  • Left Site — the time you left the job site at the end of the day.
  • Arrived at Workshop/Accom — the time you got back to workshop or accommodation.
Toggle buttons:
  • Start Workshop / Start Site — select where your working day commenced (Cl 53.a). Select Start Workshop if you were directed by your supervisor or project manager to carry out work-related tasks at your centre of employment before travelling to site. Select Start Site if you travelled directly to the worksite from home, your centre of employment (without carrying out work-related tasks), or an arranged meeting point.
  • Finish Workshop / Finish Site — select where your working day ended. Select Finish Workshop if you were directed by your supervisor or project manager to return to your centre of employment to carry out work-related tasks (e.g. unloading a trailer, preparing for a job the following day). Select Finish Site if your working day ended at the worksite and you travelled home or to accommodation.
  • Weekday / Weekend / Pub Holiday — select the day type. Weekday pays NT then OT. Weekend/PH pays double time for all work hours with a 4-hour minimum (Cl 62.3.a), or 2-hour minimum if you were notified 24h+ in advance (Cl 62.3.b). PH also includes 8 hours NT (Public Holiday) entitlement — applicable only on a day you were rostered to work (s116 FW Act).
  • Company Car — select if a company vehicle was provided or offered. If unselected, Fares Allowance (FTR) applies. Selecting Company Car also makes TK1 and TK2 own-vehicle km allowances ineligible (Cl 64.3.b — the $0.57/km is not paid when the employee travels in company transport). The calculator enforces this by disabling the TK1 and TK2 checkboxes while Company Car is on.
  • Incidentals-1 — select when staying at accommodation on a distant job (>20 min from urban area, employer pays for accom without meals). All accom-to-site travel is paid. TAT and Fares do not apply for accom↔site legs. First/Last Day tick preserves TAT/Fares for the standard workshop↔site leg (Cl 64.5.b).
  • Other ▼ — expands to reveal the less-common flags below (Unnotified OT, RDO, Callout).
  • Unnotified OT — select if you were required to work 2+ hours overtime without being notified the previous day. Triggers the $15.50 meal allowance (any day — Cl 60.1.a).
  • RDO — select when working a scheduled Rostered Day Off. Treats the day as a Saturday-equivalent penalty day (Cl 62.3.a): DT rate for all work hours, 4-hour minimum (or 2-hour with 24h+ notice).
  • Callout — select when recalled to site after hours (Cl 63.a). All work + travel is bundled at the appropriate OT rate with a 4-hour minimum. TT/TT+/TAT/Fares are suppressed — travel is already at OT. Not modelled: Cl 63.e/f on-call-roster variants (3h / 2h mins) and the Cl 63.a.iii TK1 $0.57/km own-vehicle allowance; enter these manually via Extra Allowances.
  • Scheduled (24h+ notice) — when working a weekend/PH/RDO that was planned 24h+ in advance, minimum drops from 4h to 2h (Cl 62.3.b).
Key EA rules:
  • Ordinary hours are 6am–6pm (Cl 55.a). Work outside this window is overtime regardless of total hours worked.
  • Travel Boundary: the first 30 minutes of travel each way falls inside the 30-min Travel Boundary (Cl 64.1.a). It isn't paid as TT — instead it's compensated by the daily Fares Allowance (FTR, $14.61/day, Cl 64.1.b) when eligible. Under Inc-1 the boundary doesn't apply because accommodation becomes the centre of employment (Cl 64.5.a).
  • Weekday overtime: first 2 hours at time-and-a-half (TH), then double time (DT) (Cl 62.2.a.i).
  • Weekend / PH / RDO overtime: all hours at double time (Cl 62.2.a.ii/iii) with a 4-hour minimum (Cl 62.3.a), or 2-hour minimum with 24h+ notice (Cl 62.3.b).
  • Meal break (Cl 60.a): 30-minute unpaid lunch for shifts of 5–12 hours. Shifts under 5 hours have no meal deduction. Shifts of 12+ hours get a paid meal break.
  • Early travel (Cl 64.3.d): cross the boundary before 0630hrs AND have a site more than 1 hour beyond the boundary → claim 30 min TT and the ordinary workday is deemed to start at 0700 (conditional on safe driving).
  • Rounding (Cl 53.b): time is rounded to 15-minute intervals. 7 minutes rounds down; 8 minutes rounds up to 15.

Work codes:
  • NT — Normal Time. Your ordinary 8-hour day paid at the base rate (only applies on pure weekdays; weekend/PH/RDO days pay DT from minute one).
  • NT (RDO, AL or LWOP) — Shortfall row. If you worked less than 8 hours on a pure weekday, the gap to 8 hours must be covered by a Rostered Day Off, Annual Leave, or Leave Without Pay (LWOP needs management approval). Doesn't fire on weekend/PH/RDO/Callout days — those have different minimums.
  • NT (Public Holiday) — 8 hours paid automatically as your PH entitlement, in addition to DT for hours actually worked.
  • TH — Time and a Half. The first 2 hours of weekday overtime (Cl 62.2.a.i). Also any weekday work that falls outside 6am–6pm gets counted toward TH/DT per Cl 55.a.
  • DT — Double Time. Weekday overtime beyond the first 2 hours of TH (Cl 62.2.a.i), or all work hours on weekends, public holidays, or RDO days (Cl 62.2.a.ii/iii + 62.3.a).
Travel codes:
  • TT (Morning) — Travel Time at single rate. Morning travel outside the 30-min boundary on a weekday (Cl 64.3.c.i). Paid regardless of time of day — travel rate is not affected by the 6am–6pm ordinary-hours window, which only applies to work.
  • TT+ (Morning) — Travel Time at time-and-a-half. Applies to all morning travel outside the boundary on weekends, public holidays, or RDO days (Cl 64.3.c.iii). On weekdays morning travel is never TT+ — per Cl 64.3.d the weekday TT+ rate is return-only.
  • TT (Return) — Return travel at single rate, when the day's work + outside-boundary travel hasn't exceeded 8 hours (Cl 64.3.c.i).
  • TT+ (Return) — Return travel at time-and-a-half. Applies when the day's total work + outside-boundary travel exceeds 8 hours (Cl 64.3.c.ii), or always on weekends, public holidays, or RDO days (Cl 64.3.c.iii).
Allowance codes:
  • TAT — Travel Time Allowance (Cl 64.2). A flat daily unit equal to 25% of your all-purpose hourly rate, payable when your working day commences at a worksite. Not payable under Inc-1 accom↔site travel or when the early-travel rule fires. One unit per day maximum.
  • Fares (FTR) — Fares Allowance (Cl 64.1). $14.61/day when you travel in your own time/vehicle and no company transport was offered. Covers the 30-min Travel Boundary portion.
  • Meal Allow ($15.50) — Meal Allowance for unnotified overtime of 2+ hours (Cl 60.1.a). Any day type. Rate increases to $16.41 from 01/07/2026 and $20.00 from 01/07/2027.
Interpretation notes:
  • Rounding — each component is rounded to 15 min (Cl 53.b: 7 min rounds down, 8 min rounds up). Rounding each component separately can differ from rounding totals only, by up to ±15 min.
  • Public Holiday (8h NT) — auto-added when PH is selected. Under s116 FW Act, PH NT only applies if you were rostered to work that day; untick PH if it falls on a non-rostered day.
  • Casual rates — the calculator uses full-time treatment. Casual PH pays at double time and a half (2.5× base, Cl 62.2.a.iv); not auto-calculated here. Apply the uplift manually if you're casual.
  • Early-travel weekend/PH (Appendix Ex 23–25) — the 30-min TT+ and TAT-suppression rule for weekend/PH early starts isn't auto-detected (weekday only). If your weekend early-start fits Ex 22–25, manually reduce the forward TT+ to 30 min and untick TAT.
