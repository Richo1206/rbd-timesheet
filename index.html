Google Maps Travel Tool
How to use: This tool helps you verify your travel time using Google Maps.
  • Select your start location — Devonport Office, Hobart Office, or Custom. If Custom, start typing the address or place name and pick from the Google Autocomplete dropdown (accommodation, other RBD office, meeting point, etc.).
  • Start typing the destination (site name or address) and pick from the Google Autocomplete dropdown — this gives the most reliable lookup. If the place you need isn't listed, type a street address or town + state instead.
  • Tap Calculate time to destination to auto-fill Travel Hrs/Mins using Google Distance Matrix, including current traffic.
  • Or tap Verify Route to open Google Maps in a new tab and check the route visually — useful for sanity-checking the auto-filled time.
  • The calculator uses Travel Hrs/Mins to auto-set your "Arrived on Site" time based on when you left.

Time Entries
How to fill this in:
  • Left Workshop/Accom — the time you left the workshop or accommodation.
  • Arrived on Site — the time you arrived at the job site.
  • Left Site — the time you left the job site at the end of the day.
  • Arrived at Workshop/Accom — the time you got back to workshop or accommodation.
Toggle buttons:
  • Start Workshop / Start Site — select where your working day commenced (Cl 53.a). Select Start Workshop if you were directed by your supervisor or project manager to carry out work-related tasks at your centre of employment before travelling to site. Select Start Site if you travelled directly to the worksite from home, your centre of employment (without carrying out work-related tasks), or an arranged meeting point.
  • Finish Workshop / Finish Site — select where your working day ended. Select Finish Workshop if you were directed by your supervisor or project manager to return to your centre of employment to carry out work-related tasks (e.g. unloading a trailer, preparing for a job the following day). Select Finish Site if your working day ended at the worksite and you travelled home or to accommodation.
  • Weekday / Weekend / Pub Holiday — select the day type. Weekday pays NT then OT. Weekend/PH pays double time for all work hours with a 4-hour minimum (Cl 62.3.a), or 2-hour minimum if you were notified 24h+ in advance (Cl 62.3.b). PH also includes 8 hours NT (Public Holiday) entitlement — applicable only on a day you were rostered to work (s116 FW Act).
  • Company Car — select if a company vehicle was provided or offered. If unselected, Fares Allowance (FTR) applies.
  • Incidentals-1 — select when staying at accommodation on a distant job (>20 min from urban area, employer pays for accom without meals). All accom-to-site travel is paid. TAT and Fares do not apply for accom↔site legs. First/Last Day tick preserves TAT/Fares for the standard workshop↔site leg (Cl 64.5.b).
  • Other ▼ — expands to reveal the less-common flags below (Unnotified OT, RDO, Callout).
  • Unnotified OT — select if you were required to work 2+ hours overtime without being notified the previous day. Triggers the $15.50 meal allowance (any day — Cl 60.1.a).
  • RDO — select when working a scheduled Rostered Day Off. Treats the day as a Saturday-equivalent penalty day (Cl 62.3.a): DT rate for all work hours, 4-hour minimum (or 2-hour with 24h+ notice).
  • Callout — select when recalled to site after hours (Cl 63.a). All work + travel is bundled at the appropriate OT rate with a 4-hour minimum. TT/TT+/TAT/Fares are suppressed — travel is already at OT. Not modelled: Cl 63.e/f on-call-roster variants (3h / 2h mins) and the Cl 63.a.iii TK1 $0.57/km own-vehicle allowance; enter these manually via Extra Allowances.
  • Scheduled (24h+ notice) — when working a weekend/PH/RDO that was planned 24h+ in advance, minimum drops from 4h to 2h (Cl 62.3.b).
Key rules:
  • Ordinary hours are 6am–6pm (Cl 55.a). Work outside this window is overtime.
  • The first 30 minutes of travel each way is unpaid (Travel Boundary, Cl 64.1) unless INC-1 is active.
  • Overtime on weekdays: first 2 hours at time-and-a-half (TH), then double time (DT) (Cl 62.2.a.i).
  • If you work 12+ hours, your lunch break is paid (Cl 60.a).

Understanding your timesheet codes:
  • NT — Normal Time. Your ordinary 8-hour day paid at the base rate.
  • NT (RDO, AL or LWOP) — Hours short of a full 8-hour NT day. Must be covered by a Rostered Day Off, Annual Leave, or Leave Without Pay (LWOP requires management approval).
  • NT (Public Holiday) — 8 hours paid automatically as your Public Holiday entitlement, in addition to DT for hours worked.
  • TH — Time and a Half. The first 2 hours of weekday overtime (Cl 62.2.a.i).
  • DT — Double Time. Weekday overtime beyond the first 2 hours, or all work hours on weekends/public holidays.
  • TT (Morning) — Travel Time (single rate). Paid travel from workshop/accom to site, outside the 30-min Travel Boundary, during ordinary hours (6am–6pm).
  • TT+ (Morning) — Travel Time (time and a half). Morning travel outside the boundary that falls before 6am, or all travel on weekends/PH.
  • TT (Return) — Return travel at single rate, when total work + travel hasn't exceeded 8 hours.
  • TT+ (Return) — Return travel at time and a half, when total work + travel exceeds 8 hours, or on weekends/PH.
  • TAT — Travel Time Allowance. A flat daily unit (25% of hourly rate) when your day starts at a worksite, not the workshop.
  • Fares (FTR) — Fares Allowance ($14.61/day). Paid when you use your own vehicle and no company car was offered.
  • Meal Allow ($15.50) — Meal Allowance for unnotified overtime of 2+ hours (Cl 60.1.a). Note: this rate increases to $16.41 from 01/07/2026 and $20.00 from 01/07/2027.
Interpretation notes:
  • Rounding — each component is rounded to 15 min (Cl 53.b). Small slivers across several components can differ from rounding the totals only (+/- 15 min).
  • Public Holiday (8h NT) — auto-added when PH is selected. Under s116 FW Act, PH NT only applies if you were rostered to work that day; untick PH if it falls on a non-rostered day.
  • Casual rates — the calculator uses full-time treatment. Casual PH pays at DT+half (Cl 62.2.a.iv); not auto-calculated here.
  • Early-travel (weekend/PH) — examples 23/24/25 show reduced 30-min TT+ and no TAT for weekend/PH early starts. Not auto-detected here (weekday only); if your weekend early-start fits Ex 22-25, manually reduce the forward TT+ to 30 min and remove TAT.
